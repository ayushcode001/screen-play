Format Guide

🎬 Scene Heading INT/EXT

Also called a "slug line". Tells us WHERE and WHEN a scene takes place. Always in UPPERCASE.

INT. DELHI MARKET — DAY
EXT. MUMBAI BEACH — NIGHT
INT. TRAIN COMPARTMENT — DUSK

📝 Action Line

Describe only what the camera SEES and EARS can HEAR. Write in present tense. No inner thoughts!

❌ Priya thinks about him.
✅ Priya stares at his photo, eyes filling with tears.

🎭 Character Cue

Name in ALL CAPS, centered. Add (V.O.) for voice-over narration, (O.S.) if character is off-screen.

VIKRAM
PRIYA (V.O.)
SHARMA JI (O.S.)

💬 Dialogue

Centered block below the character name. Can mix Hindi & English (Hinglish) naturally!

"Ek baar jo maine commitment kar li... toh phir main apne aap ki bhi nahi sunta."

( ) Parenthetical

Acting direction in brackets, placed between character name and dialogue. Use sparingly!

(crying)
(whispering, afraid)
(looking away)

⟶ Transition

Used to indicate how we move from one scene to the next. Right-aligned, in caps.

🎵 Song Cue HINDI FILM

Unique to Hindi films! Marks where a song-and-dance sequence begins. Write song name in caps.

SONG: "DIL DHADAKNE DO"
— A dream sequence begins. The world turns golden —

💡 Golden Rules

• 1 page ≈ 1 minute of screen time
• Introduce characters in CAPS on first appearance
• Write for the audience's eyes, not the reader's imagination
• Hindi films average 150–170 pages (2.5–3 hrs)
